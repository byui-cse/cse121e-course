Grading and Feedback

  • Since there are no graded assignments in this course, you will need to be proactive in reviewing and providing constructive feedback on students' tasks. (See the General Teaching Notes.) Check the results from the W03 Quiz with the Tasks to determine if any students who completed the quiz did not turn in a task submission. If students do not submit their tasks but receive points from the quiz, you should reach out to them to ask for the submission so you can check their understanding. You are allowed to remove their quiz points if they do not respond to you or refuse to submit the task for review and feedback.

Week 04 Wrap Up - Release Friday

Dear Erlang Students,

This last week we've been learning how to spawn processes and send messages between processes. If you're encountering any questions or difficulty with understanding the reading material or the weekly task, I would encourage you to look for additional resources to help you learn. In addition to the documentation and tutorials available at https://www.erlang.org/, I also recommend looking at the free online book "learn you some Erlang for great good!". This is an excellent resource with more examples of how to use Erlang, in an easy to read format.

For Week 05, we'll be continuing with the concept of message passing and spawning processes, but we'll also add the concept of maintaining state. Think of "state" as all the things the program needs to remember or keep track of. Erlang has an interesting way of maintaining state throughout the lifecycle of a process. As you do the reading, make sure to try out the example code and play with it until you understand it.

Final Project

You should start thinking about a potential final project, which will be due in two weeks. For a lookahead, see the Week 06 Task. This will give you some ideas of projects you might consider. Remember, the final project is required in order to pass this course. If you have an idea you would like to run by me, feel free to send me a message on Teams or by email.
